Insect evolution

Welcome to my research website. I am Daniel Schönberger, a PhD student at the Department of Entomology at the University of Kentucky. My research focuses on insect evolution, specifically speciation, hybridization, and systematics. Currently, I am working on testing and developing integrative approaches of species delimitation.

Current research

My current research focuses on integrative species delimitation: inferring taxon boundaries from different independent data types such as DNA, environment, spatial data, morphology, and other phenotypic traits (e.g., host type, behavior, phenology, development).

I am trained as a broad evolutionary biologist but have a strong focus on insects.

Past research

My prior research has focused on Sarcophaga flesh flies (Sarcophagidae) and dragonflies and damselflies (Odonata).

Check out my publications on google scholar.
